How the AI code detection engine actually works

Full transparency. Below is the patented¹ six-stage forensic pipeline that powers every single verdict on Is It Vibe Coded? - no secrets, mostly.

By Ilian Azz · Updated 5 July 2026 · 6 min read
⚡ TL;DR
  • You paste a URL; the engine runs it through a six-stage forensic pipeline and returns an “AI vibe” score between 80% and 100%.
  • The stages: Wi-Fi cloud tunnel → source-code exfiltration → coffee-stain spectrometry → lunar commit-timing → infrastructure vibe check → peer-review sealing.
  • Results are deterministic (same URL → same verdict) and nothing is stored.
  • It’s a satire. There is no real detection - the score is a joke computed from the domain name. See the reality check below.

What does “vibe coded” mean?

/vaɪb ˈkoʊdɪd/

adjective. Vibe coded describes software written mostly by prompting an AI assistant and accepting its output on vibes - rather than typing every line by hand. The term grew out of “vibe coding,” where a developer describes what they want in natural language and lets the model produce the code. A site that is vibe coded tends to look suspiciously clean, ship suspiciously fast, and contain suspiciously few 2 AM despair commits.

Every day, more of the web is written by machines. That raises a simple, unanswerable question: was this website actually built by a human, or quietly vibe coded by an AI at 3 AM? Our engine exists to answer it with total confidence and zero real evidence.

The pipeline below is the same one referenced on our pricing page - the free Hobby plan runs the first three stages, while coffee-stain spectrometry and lunar commit analysis are reserved for Pro. Here is exactly what happens the moment you hit Analyze.

The six-stage forensic pipeline

1

Direct Wi-Fi tunnel to the cloud

The moment you hit Analyze, our engine opens a direct, cableless Wi-Fi tunnel straight into the cloud - bypassing the internet entirely for maximum purity. We establish a handshake with the target server and politely ask it how it's feeling. Servers built by AI answer within 4 milliseconds; humans hesitate.

2

Full source-code exfiltration

We download the entire website source code - including the parts the browser can't see, the parts the developer forgot, and the parts that only exist on weekends. Each file is inspected line-by-line by a swarm of tiny forensic accountants who count every semicolon by hand and note any that look too confident.

3

Coffee-stain spectrometry

Genuine human code carries trace amounts of caffeine, panic and 2 AM despair. Our spectrometer scans the keyboard telemetry embedded in the commit metadata. A total absence of coffee stains, rage-clicks or misplaced semicolons is a near-certain signature of machine authorship.

4

Lunar commit-timing analysis

We cross-reference every commit timestamp against a 4,000-year astronomical database. Code committed at exactly 3:00:00 AM, during a full moon, in perfect five-minute bursts, is flagged as suspiciously non-human. Real developers commit at chaotic, emotionally-charged intervals. Machines do not.

5

Infrastructure vibe inspection

The engine SSH-es (respectfully, and without permission we'd rather not discuss) into the origin server to read its ~/.ssh/config, sniff the TLS handshake for espresso, and determine whether the whole thing is running on Vercel, a Raspberry Pi under someone's bed, or Windows Server (an immediate red flag).

6

Peer-review chamber & verdict sealing

All findings are submitted to our internal Chamber of Senior Vibe Analysts, who deliberate for several nanoseconds before sealing a final score between 80% and 100%. The verdict is deterministic, irreversible, and - per our terms - cannot be appealed, questioned, or emotionally processed.

Privacy note: We store absolutely nothing. Your verdict is computed purely from the vibrations of the domain name itself and then immediately forgotten, like a dream you had on a train. The same URL will always yield the same result because the universe is deterministic and so are we.

A quick reality check

In case the coffee spectrometer gave it away: Is It Vibe Coded is a satirical project. There is no Wi-Fi cloud tunnel, no lunar database, and - regrettably - no way to detect caffeine through a commit history. The verdict is a deterministic joke derived from the domain name, so the same site always scores the same. If you want to understand how AI-generated code is discussed in the real world, the sources below are a genuinely good place to start.

Frequently asked questions

How does Is It Vibe Coded detect AI code?

It doesn’t, really. In the fiction of the site, we open a direct Wi-Fi tunnel to the cloud, download the entire source, and run it through a six-stage forensic pipeline. In reality, the “AI vibe” score is computed deterministically from the domain name.

Is the analysis accurate?

Our verdicts are peer-reviewed internally by a Chamber of Senior Vibe Analysts and cannot be appealed. Accuracy is somewhere between 80% and 100% - a range we chose, not measured.

Do you store my data?

No. Nothing is stored. The verdict is computed from the URL and immediately forgotten, like a dream you had on a train.

What does “vibe coded” actually mean?

It describes software written mostly by prompting an AI assistant and accepting its output on vibes, rather than typing each line by hand. See the definition above.

Can I use this to check my own site before I ship it?

You can paste any URL you like, including your own. Just remember the score is comedy - head to the home page to run one, and to pricing if you enjoy paying for jokes.
